# Pit System

The Pit System exists to reduce avoidable pit-lane mistakes. It covers pit entry guidance, pit box approach, pit timing and loss, pit commands, fuel/tyre control, stop debriefs, and track markers.

The first thing you should do with a new circuit is validate the pit markers and pit lane loss. Once those are correct, the rest of the Pit System becomes much more reliable. A clean limiter-speed drive-through is the best way to learn pit loss. For each car, also review pit entry deceleration and buffer settings; a braking cue that is excellent in one car may be too early or too late in another.

Treat pit service regulations as Strategy assumptions, not automatic discoveries. If you are modelling IMSA or NEC service timing, choose that deliberately in Strategy/Race Presets and keep the profile timing values realistic.

Trust pit guidance when markers, pit loss, service assumptions, and car-specific entry settings have been validated. Question it after messy pit entries, unusual traffic, incorrect marker learning, bad pit-loss evidence, or any command feedback that suggests iRacing did not accept the intended fuel/tyre command.
