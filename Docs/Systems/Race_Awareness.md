# Race Awareness

Race Awareness answers a different question from Traffic Awareness: what is happening in the race? It is about same-class rivals, race order, H2H race context, League Class, LapRef/reference comparisons, and position awareness.

Use Race Awareness when you care about who you are racing rather than who is physically closest. In multiclass sessions especially, the nearest car may not be your real race threat. Race Awareness helps separate local traffic from strategic race context.

Configure League Class deliberately when class grouping matters. Treat H2H Race and LapRef as comparison tools: they are most useful when the underlying opponent/session context is healthy and the selected class/race basis matches the session you are running.

Trust Race Awareness when opponent data is healthy, class configuration is correct, and the race context matches what iRacing is reporting. Question it when class data is missing, League Class rules are untested, the session has unusual ordering, or you are looking at a local traffic problem that belongs to Traffic Awareness instead.

For user-facing comparison workflow, read [H2H System](../Features/H2H_System.md).
