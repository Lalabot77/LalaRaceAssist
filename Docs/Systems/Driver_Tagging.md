# Driver Tagging

Driver Tagging helps you recognize known drivers by adding awareness categories such as friend, team, or bad-style context. It is a presentation and awareness tool, not a race-control or strategy system.

Use tags for drivers you want to spot quickly on supported surfaces. Keep the list tidy and current; old tags can be more confusing than no tags at all. If imported names or identifiers do not match the current session, review the source before trusting the visual cue.

The important boundary is that tags do not change official race data. They should help you notice people, not change class resolution, opponent ordering, strategy math, pit logic, or the truth of the timing tower.

Trust Driver Tagging when the identity match is clear and the tag list is maintained. Question it when names differ, imports are stale, or a tag appears on a driver you did not expect.

Driver Tagging appears in presentation systems such as traffic awareness styling. Technical consumption details are documented where needed in [CarSA](../Subsystems/Traffic_Awareness/CarSA.md) and [Plugin UI Tooltips](../Internal/Plugin_UI_Tooltips.md).
